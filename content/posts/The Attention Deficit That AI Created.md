# The Attention Deficit That AI Created

The cost of creating just dropped to zero. The cost of consuming never changed.

Every sales team, marketing department, and founder with a ChatGPT subscription is producing more content than ever before. More emails, more LinkedIn posts, more sequences, more "thought leadership." And yet the people on the receiving end still have the same 24 hours, the same two eyes, and the same increasingly frayed attention span they had two years ago.

We've solved the supply problem. Nobody is talking about the demand side.

## The Asymmetry

Before generative AI, writing a cold email took real effort. You had to think about your prospect, draft something coherent, maybe revise it. That friction was a natural quality filter. If something wasn't worth 15 minutes of your time to write, it probably wasn't worth sending.

Now that same email takes 30 seconds and a prompt. The marginal unit of effort to create has collapsed to near zero. And when the cost of production drops, production explodes. That's basic economics.

But the marginal cost of consumption didn't change. Reading that email still takes your prospect 30 seconds of attention they won't get back. One side of the equation got 100x easier. The other side stayed exactly the same.

## What This Looks Like in Practice

Open rates are declining. LinkedIn engagement is harder to earn. Prospects are building faster, more aggressive filters for what deserves their time. Not because your messaging got worse, but because everyone's volume got higher.

The irony is brutal: the tool that made it easier than ever to reach people is simultaneously making it harder to actually reach them.

When every competitor has the same AI tools and follows the same "more volume" logic, the channel gets flooded. Your prospect who used to get 20 cold emails a week now gets 80. They didn't develop 4x the patience. They developed a faster delete finger.

## Respect the Reader

Paul Graham [put it perfectly](https://x.com/paulg/status/2023020990943600849): "When you write something intended to be read by an important person, go through it and cut every unnecessary word. The reader of anything you publish is an important person."

That advice predates generative AI, but it has never been more relevant. When anyone can produce infinite content at zero cost, the discipline to cut, to tighten, to only publish what's genuinely worth someone's time becomes the differentiator.

AI makes it trivially easy to add words. The hard, human work is knowing which words to remove.

## What Actually Wins Now

If AI has commoditized creation, then the things it can't easily replicate become the new premium.

**Genuine insight over generated content.** There's a growing gap between content that says something and content that sounds like something. Readers can feel the difference, even if they can't articulate it. A real point of view, a lived experience, a specific opinion. Those are hard to prompt-engineer.

**Relevance beyond personalization tokens.** Dropping a prospect's company name and job title into a template passed for personalization in 2023. In 2025, it reads as a slightly more sophisticated mail merge. Real relevance means understanding someone's actual problem and articulating it better than they can.

**Curation over creation.** In a world drowning in content, helping others figure out what's worth paying attention to is arguably more valuable than creating yet another piece.

## The Question Has Changed

Attention hasn't scaled. It won't. No amount of AI advancement will give your prospect more hours in the day or a higher tolerance for being sold to.

The question is no longer "how do I create more?" It's "how do I earn attention that's scarcer than it's ever been?" And that's a problem AI alone can't solve.
