A collection from winter travels -- cold air, sharp light, and the way snow simplifies everything it touches.

## Cities in Snow

The first snowfall turns any city into something quieter. Familiar streets become unfamiliar. Sound dampens. People move differently.

![Ornate building corner on a snowy city street](https://unsplash.com/photos/ornate-building-corner-on-a-snowy-city-street-hdgHGw5EqIc)

![Green sign pointing to Las Palmas with palm trees](https://unsplash.com/photos/green-sign-pointing-to-las-palmas-with-palm-trees-knuQ_9VQ9SQ)

## Water and Light

Even in winter, there are pockets of warmth -- canal reflections, golden hour stretched long by the low sun.

![A boat travels on a canal in front of buildings](https://unsplash.com/photos/a-boat-travels-on-a-canal-in-front-of-buildings-t8OzFHgBjYk)

## Mountains

The mountains in winter are a study in contrast. Hard rock against soft snow. Warm sunset light against cold blue shadow.

![Sunrise over a snow covered mountain range](https://unsplash.com/photos/sunrise-over-a-snow-covered-mountain-range-c1ttYPlApAw)

![Snow capped mountains bathed in soft sunset light](https://unsplash.com/photos/snow-capped-mountains-bathed-in-soft-sunset-light-MD3FL3s4z3s)

## After Dark

The best winter nights are the clear ones. No clouds, no light pollution, just the Milky Way stretched across a frozen sky.
