A collection from winter travels -- cold air, sharp light, and the way snow simplifies everything it touches.

## Cities in Snow

The first snowfall turns any city into something quieter. Familiar streets become unfamiliar. Sound dampens. People move differently.

![Ornate building corner on a snowy city street](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1771600093943-0a5370e845f5?q=80&w=2000&auto=format&fit=crop)

![Green sign pointing to Las Palmas with palm trees](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1771673023573-826a5d3546e5?q=80&w=2000&auto=format&fit=crop)

## Water and Light

Even in winter, there are pockets of warmth -- canal reflections, golden hour stretched long by the low sun.

![A boat travels on a canal in front of buildings](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1770037367722-5444567fcb52?q=80&w=2000&auto=format&fit=crop)

## Mountains

The mountains in winter are a study in contrast. Hard rock against soft snow. Warm sunset light against cold blue shadow.

![Sunrise over a snow covered mountain range](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1771140769180-9982d19dc0c7?q=80&w=2000&auto=format&fit=crop)

## After Dark

The best winter nights are the clear ones. No clouds, no light pollution, just the Milky Way stretched across a frozen sky.
